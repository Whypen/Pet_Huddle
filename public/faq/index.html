About Huddle

What is Huddle?
Huddle is a pet safety, local pet community, and pet care app that brings urgent alerts, trusted local connection, and organised pet records into one place.
What is Huddle used for?
People use Huddle to protect pets locally, connect with nearby pet people, find trusted pet care, and keep pet records such as vaccinations, medications, notes, and care history organised.
Is Huddle free?
Yes. Most core Huddle safety and community features are free. Paid plans and add-ons are for people who want more reach, more visibility, or extra tools, and live availability can vary by market and app version.
Who is Huddle for?
Huddle is for pet owners, animal lovers, rescue volunteers, foster networks, shelters, and trusted local pet carers. It is designed for everyday pet life as well as urgent local situations.
What makes Huddle different from other pet care platforms?
Huddle combines pet safety, local pet community, trusted pet care, groups, chats, and pet records in one app instead of solving only one problem. That lets people respond to urgent situations, connect locally, and manage pet information in the same place.
Is Huddle only for dogs and cats?
No. Huddle supports many species, including rabbits, birds, guinea pigs, hamsters, reptiles, fish, and other pets. Service availability still depends on the provider and area.
Do I need an account to use Huddle?
Yes. Huddle requires an account because account-based access makes the community more accountable and helps reduce spam, fake accounts, and low-trust activity.

Lost Pets, Alerts, and Emergencies

How do Huddle Broadcast Alerts work?
Huddle Broadcast Alerts send urgent pet-related alerts to nearby users based on the selected reach. They are designed for situations where speed, coverage, and location matter more than relying on one post, one group, or one chat alone.
What is the Huddle Live Map?
The Huddle Live Map gives local pet situations a real location, which makes lost pets, stray animals, hazards, and urgent local situations easier to understand and act on than a general feed post with no exact place attached.
What are the pin types on the Huddle Live Map?
The main map pin types are Lost Pet, Stray Animal, Caution, and Others. Lost Pet is for missing pets, Stray Animal is for loose or found animals, Caution is for local safety risks, and Others is for urgent or relevant animal situations that do not fit the other categories.
What should I do if I lose my pet?
Open the Huddle Live Map, drop a Lost Pet pin, add a recent photo and accurate details, and send a Broadcast Alert. That gives the case local map visibility, nearby awareness, and wider community response in one flow.
How do I report a stray or injured animal near me?
Open the Huddle Live Map, add a Stray Animal or Others pin, include the exact location and details, and send a Broadcast Alert if urgent help is needed. Huddle is designed to keep location, visibility, and community response together.
How do I warn nearby users about poison, hazards, or a dangerous area?
Use a Caution pin in the Huddle Live Map and include the exact location, the exact risk, and a photo only if it is safe to take one. This gives nearby pet people a factual location-based warning instead of a vague general message.
How do I edit or remove a pin on the Huddle Live Map?
Open your own pin on the Huddle Live Map and choose Edit or Remove. Huddle is built so users can keep cases accurate as situations change and prevent stale alerts from confusing the community.

Community, Advice, Discover, and Groups

What is the Huddle Social Forum?
The Huddle Social Forum is a local discussion space for pet questions, rescue threads, practical advice, local conversations, and meetups. It is designed for real follow-up and local context, not just one-way reading.
Can I ask pet questions in the Huddle Social Forum?
Yes. People use it for behaviour, feeding, training, rescue, symptoms, and species-specific care, and the discussion can continue in the same thread instead of ending at a single answer.
What is Huddle Discover?
Huddle Discover helps people meet nearby pet owners, carers, and animal lovers in a more pet-focused way than general networking tools. People use it for playdates, walking partners, rescue collaboration, and local social connection.
What are Huddle Groups?
Huddle Groups move conversations from open discussion into a more focused shared space before people decide whether to share personal contact details. They are useful for local areas, rescue teams, species communities, breed communities, meetups, and other shared interests.
Can I create a private pet group on Huddle?
Yes, where the feature is available in your market and app version. Private groups are designed for rescue teams, neighbourhood circles, repeat playdates, and other smaller communities where trust and coordination matter more than open discovery.
How do I meet other pet owners near me?
People usually use Huddle Discover, the Social Forum, Groups, and Chats together. The product is designed to let people build familiarity in-app before deciding whether to move to direct contact.

Trusted Local Pet Care

What is Huddle Service?
Huddle Service is the local pet care marketplace inside Huddle. It is designed around identity verification, provider context, and community trust signals rather than anonymous listings alone.
Where can I find a trusted pet sitter near me?
Use Huddle Service to review provider profiles, the Huddle Verified Identity Badge, species experience, pricing, and availability. The goal is to make it easier to judge trust before booking, not just compare listings.
Can I find verified pet care providers near me?
Yes. Huddle Service helps people compare providers with verification, clearer profile details, pricing, availability, and community presence where provider supply exists in the area.
What is the safest way to book pet care online?
Book through Huddle Service when you want payment handling, booking support, and dispute support inside the platform flow instead of sorting money privately with someone you barely know.
Can I offer my own pet care services on Huddle?
Yes, where provider onboarding is available in your market and app version. Trusted local providers can apply to list services, set pricing and availability, and build trust over time through verification and community presence.
