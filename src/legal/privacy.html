Privacy Policy

Effective date: 25 March 2026 · Operator: Huddle

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information Huddle collects, why Huddle uses it, when Huddle shares it, how long Huddle keeps it, how Huddle transfers it, and what rights and choices users may have.

1. Scope

This Policy applies to Huddle's app, websites, legal pages, support channels, marketplace tools, social features, map features, payment flows, and related services.

2. Information Huddle collects

  • Account information, such as email address, password, phone number, date of birth, display name, and settings.
  • Profile information, such as photos, bio, preferences, pet details, service listings, and related profile content.
  • Content and communications, such as messages, posts, comments, reviews, reports, support requests, and booking details.
  • Payment and transaction information, such as subscription status, billing events, refunds, disputes, and limited payment metadata.
  • Device, app, and usage information, such as device type, app version, IP address, language, time zone, crash data, and usage data.
  • Location information where enabled.
  • Trust and safety information, such as phone-verification status, card-check status, fraud signals, moderation records, complaint records, and liveness-check results.
  • Information from third parties, such as payment providers, app stores, analytics providers, business partners, public sources, and other users where relevant.

Huddle does not intentionally store raw biometric identifiers. If Huddle uses a liveness or human-check tool, it is used only to confirm that a real person is using the service and to help prevent abuse.

3. How Huddle uses information

  • To create, operate, and secure accounts.
  • To provide profile, messaging, discovery, map, booking, payment, subscription, support, moderation, and community features.
  • To verify phone numbers, reduce fraud, and protect the platform.
  • To process purchases, subscriptions, refunds, cancellations, disputes, and support requests.
  • To personalise content, recommendations, notifications, and user experience.
  • To measure performance, fix problems, improve the service, and develop features.
  • To send service messages, updates, offers, marketing, or promotions where allowed by law.
  • To comply with law, enforce the Terms, and protect users, pets, Huddle, and the public.

4. Legal bases

Where the law requires a legal basis, Huddle relies on one or more of these: contract, legitimate interests, legal obligation, consent, or protection of vital interests.

5. When Huddle shares information

  • With vendors and service providers that help host, secure, support, analyse, verify, advertise, measure, or process payments for the service.
  • With other users when sharing is part of the feature you use, such as profiles, messages, reviews, bookings, map participation, and community posts.
  • With payment processors, billing providers, app stores, and financial partners.
  • With analytics, advertising, attribution, recommendation, or measurement partners, subject to applicable law and any required notices, choices, or consent.
  • With legal authorities or other third parties where reasonably necessary to comply with law, enforce agreements, investigate misuse, or protect rights, safety, pets, or the public.
  • With parties involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, restructuring, or sale of assets.

Huddle may also use and share de-identified or aggregated information where lawful.

6. Public and community features

Some information may be visible to other users or the public, depending on your settings and the features you use. This may include profile information, pet information, posts, comments, reviews, booking details, service listings, and map-related activity. Other users may keep or reshare content even after you remove it from Huddle.

7. Location data

If you enable location access, Huddle may use location data for nearby discovery, map participation, alerts, safety features, service availability, fraud prevention, and analytics. You can manage some location choices in the app and some in your device settings.

8. Automated tools

Huddle may use automated rules, filters, ranking tools, and fraud or safety tools to support feed order, recommendations, spam control, moderation, and platform security. Huddle may also use human review where needed.

9. Advertising, analytics, and personalisation

Huddle may use personal information for analytics, recommendations, advertising, measurement, and personalisation. Where required by law, Huddle will provide notice, consent options, opt-out choices, or other controls.

10. Payments

Payments may be handled by third-party providers. Huddle may keep limited payment metadata such as transaction identifiers, billing status, card brand, partial card details, refund status, and dispute records. Huddle does not intentionally store raw payment card numbers.

11. Retention

Huddle keeps personal information for as long as reasonably needed to provide the service, run the business, comply with law, resolve disputes, prevent fraud, and protect safety. Retention periods vary by data type, account status, and legal requirements.

12. International transfers

Huddle may process and store information outside a user's home country. Where required by law, Huddle will use appropriate safeguards for international transfers.

13. Security

Huddle uses technical, organisational, and access controls designed to protect personal information. No system is completely secure. Where required by law, Huddle will notify affected users or authorities about certain personal data breaches.

14. Age rules

Huddle is for users aged 13 and above. Some features have a higher minimum age. Discover and stranger chat are limited to users aged 16 and above. Huddle may use age information, feature gating, review, and enforcement to apply these limits.

15. Rights and choices

Depending on applicable law, users may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or receive a copy of certain personal information, withdraw consent where consent applies, and opt out of certain marketing, advertising, sale, sharing, or similar uses where applicable. For the available contact routes and request process, see the Privacy Choices page.

16. Third-party services

Huddle may link to or rely on third-party services such as app stores, maps, payment providers, messaging tools, analytics tools, and support platforms. Their own terms and privacy notices apply to their services.

17. Changes to this Policy

Huddle may update this Policy from time to time. The latest version will appear on Huddle's public legal pages.
